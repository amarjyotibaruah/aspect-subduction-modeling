# Model Descriptions

This document provides an overview of the geodynamic models developed to investigate mantle flow dynamics in subduction systems. The models are designed in a progressive framework to isolate and compare key physical processes.

---

## 1. Simple Subduction Model

A 3D subduction model representing an oceanic plate subducting beneath a continental lithosphere with a thick cratonic root. The mantle is treated as isoviscous with a viscosity of 1e20 Pa·s.

**Objective:**
To establish the reference poloidal flow pattern and thermal structure in a simplified subduction system.

**Key Features:**
- No slab-edge geometry
- No localized viscosity perturbations
- Dominant large-scale poloidal mantle circulation

---

## 2. Small-Scale Convection (SSC) Model

This model extends the baseline configuration by introducing a localized low-viscosity zone (1e18 Pa·s) beneath the lithosphere within an otherwise isoviscous mantle.

**Objective:**
To investigate the initiation and evolution of small-scale convection (SSC) and its interaction with subduction-driven flow.

**Key Processes Studied:**
- Onset of mantle instabilities beneath the lithosphere
- Interaction between SSC and background poloidal flow
- Influence of localized rheological weakening on convection

---

## 3. Slab-Edge Toroidal Flow Model

A 3D subduction model with a finite slab geometry that allows lateral mantle flow around slab edges.

**Objective:**
To analyze the development of toroidal mantle flow induced by slab-edge geometry.

**Key Features:**
- Finite slab geometry introducing lateral boundaries
- Generation of toroidal circulation around slab edges
- Strong three-dimensional mantle flow patterns

---

## Notes

- All models are implemented using ASPECT.
- Figures shown in the repository represent selected outputs from these models.
- Detailed model configurations and full parameter files are not included as they are part of ongoing research work.
